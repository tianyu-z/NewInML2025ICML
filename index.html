New In ML at ICML 2025

NewInML will be held at ICML 2025 on July 15 , 2025! Location: Vancouver Convention Center.

100% submissions will be accepted* and will get high quality reviews from reviewers. We are here to help! The submissions are non archival. Feel free to submit elsewhere in the future! We are updating the website frequently. Stay tuned! If you have any question, please contact us by: contactnewinml@gmail.com; join our Discord.

If you are interested in helping organize next NewInML, please let us know! https://forms.gle/oyedWiU9RBhrdiEi6

*Excluding submissions that are not serious. For example: blank pages, topics not in machine learning and papers completely not following the format.

Our Mission

Is this your first time to a top conference? Have you ever wanted your own work recognized by this huge and active community?

Do you encounter difficulties in polishing your ideas, experiments, paper writing, etc? Then, this session is exactly for you!

This year, we are hosting again the New in ML workshop at ICML 2025. This workshop is intended for anyone who has not published a paper at a top conference yet (e.g. ICML, NeurIPS). We invited top researchers to review your work and share their experience with you. The best papers will get oral presentations and awards!

Our biggest goal is to help you publish papers at the next ICML conference, and generally provide you with the guidance you need to contribute to ML research fully and effectively!

Within quota limits, the authors of the best accepted papers may be receiving tickets to ICML 2025, to be attributed according to merit and need.

Submission Information

  • Submission deadline: June 10, 2025 (AoE)
  • Submission link: OpenReview
  • Submission format: ICML (template)
  • Review Anonymity: Double blind
  • All requirements, including the page limit, follow ICML main conference guidelines.

This workshop WILL NOT reject any submissions. All submitted papers will receive constructive comments from reviewers, who will provide suggestions for improvement. This process allows authors to become more familiar with the submission and review workflow.
